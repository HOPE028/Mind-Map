NOMIE

F

Faith

Faith is an unshakable belief that guides us through the unseen, the unknown and the unseen. Nomi had a hard time holding on to her faith as she watched her loved ones lose theirs. She fought a long and hard battle to keep her faith alive, but by the end of it, Nomi could no longer claim to be a faithful person.

T.M

The Mouth

Hatred, anger, and sadness consumed Nomi's thoughts when she thought of the mouth. The mouth was one of the main factors that led to the downfall of her family, which was the most precious thing in the world to her.

Tr

Trudie

Mother, puzzle, friend, mystery, loved one, confusion, wife of Ray, lover of Mr. Quiring. Why oh why did she ever leave? And why did she take so long to do so?

R

Ray

Ray was simple, straightforward, and caring, the perfect complement to Trudie, a complex individual. He was someone Trudie could rely on without reservation and have complete faith in. However, Ray had a hard time balancing his faith and his family, and in the end, he lost both. It was a difficult decision to make and ultimately caused him to lose both the most important things to him.

Fa

Family

For Nomi, life was uncomplicated when she had her family. She had a clear purpose and goal. Without her family, Nomi yearned for their return, so she could go back to how things were before. Her ultimate goal was to be reunited with her family in heaven, but the sad reality was that she will not have the happy reunion she had envisioned.

M.Q

Mr. Quiring

The teacher. The cheater. The lover of school. The lover of Trudie. Mr. Quiring represented one of the core reasons Trudie left. Nomi knew that all along. All the times she was kicked out of his class. All the times he yelled at her. All the times he hoped she did better and said "I know things are rough at home".

Fr

Freedom

In Nomi's town, the concept of freedom is foreign, it is believed that only the afterlife would give true freedom. Growing up, Nomi took this belief to heart, but as she grew older and her family was gone, she began to lose her adherence to it. With the loss of her family, many of the societal restrictions that had governed her life were lifted. She began to engage in activities that were forbidden by her town, and she did not care that they were against the law. She had nothing to lose.

Sh

Shame

Shame was always closely tied to the church in Nomi's little town. It was something that was inflicted on individuals or families as a form of punishment, often in the form of shunning and excommunication. These actions had a profound and long-lasting impact on Nomi's family, as they struggled to cope with the shame, isolation and rejection imposed upon them. The result could be seen in the absence of Trudie and Tash, among other things, as they were forced to leave the community and their loved ones.
